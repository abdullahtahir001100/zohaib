Akiro Sato: The Quiet Master of Kyoto

Akiro Sato began his apprenticeship at the age of ten, not with a pen, but with the meticulous restoration of ancient scrolls. This early exposure to the historical preservation of art instilled in him a profound respect for materials and craftsmanship. He spent years experimenting with traditional ink recipes, eventually developing the archival mineral inks included in this set—pigments that promise to hold their depth and luster for centuries.

His workshop, nestled in the historic Higashiyama district of Kyoto, is less a factory and more a sanctuary. He believes that the energy and intention put into creating a tool transfer to the user's work. This Masterpiece Set is his first widely available collection, representing a distillation of everything he has learned about balance, feel, and timeless elegance. Purchasing this set is not just buying a tool; it is acquiring a piece of Sato's dedication to the craft.

Protecting Your Investment

Care Instructions:

To maintain the integrity of your rosewood pen holder, avoid prolonged exposure to direct sunlight and extreme humidity. After use, wipe the holder clean with a soft, dry cloth. Nibs should be rinsed immediately with clean water and thoroughly dried to prevent corrosion. Never leave the nib soaking in water.
